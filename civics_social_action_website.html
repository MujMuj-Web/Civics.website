Our philosophy

Every person deserves fairness, safety, and equal access to justice. Strong institutions are necessary for peace, democracy, and human rights.

⚖

Fairness

🛡

📣

Awareness

Educating students is one of the first steps toward long-term change.

The problem

Many countries struggle with weak peace, justice, and strong institutions. This leads to corruption, unfair laws, political instability, and limited access to justice. The following summary is based on your planner content.

What is happening?

Weak or corrupt governments, poverty, conflict, and inequality can damage courts, police systems, and public trust. In some places, people cannot rely on their leaders or legal systems to protect them.

This causes human rights violations, unfair treatment, and cycles of instability that can last for years.

Who is affected?

  • People living in war zones
  • Minority groups facing discrimination
  • Journalists and human rights activists
  • Families who cannot access fair courts
  • Countries affected by refugees and instability

Impact on Canada and the world

This issue matters beyond one country. It can affect migration, public services, trade, security, and the health of democracy everywhere.

🌍

Global instability

Weak institutions can make conflict worse and make international cooperation harder.

🏠

Canadian communities

Refugees and migration affect population growth, public services, and how communities respond.

🤝

Why students should care

Fairness, equality, and trust in institutions are important in Canada too.

What is already being done

The United Nations, Amnesty International, and Transparency International all work on peace, human rights, and anti-corruption. Their work has helped raise awareness, but the problem is still serious.

What we will do

Goal 1: Awareness

Reach at least 500 students with presentations, announcements, posters, and social media.

Goal 2: Fundraising

Raise $500 over 2 years through school events and donation drives.

How success is measured

Track attendance, engagement, money raised, and event participation.

Funding sources

Two realistic ways our organization could receive funding:

  • School fundraisers such as snack sales, basketball tournaments, intramurals, or donation drives
  • A GoFundMe or online donation campaign to support education and human rights organizations

Funds would support workshops, awareness materials, and organizations that protect human rights and fight corruption.
